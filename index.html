Why I Built This

After Years Behind the Lens,
It Is Time to Give Back

I did not start photography with a fancy camera or a mentor. I started with a secondhand phone, curiosity that would not quit, and the stubborn belief that I could build something from nothing. Lagos taught me that lesson early. Before photography, I was hustling as a dispatch rider, running a small printing business, learning what it meant to trade hours for survival. The streets were my first classroom.

Then I picked up a camera. And everything shifted.

Over the years, I went from that first uncertain click to delivering excellent service across photography, cinematography, and production. I have shot weddings where the power failed and I had to light the entire hall with two speedlights and sheer willpower. I have directed productions where the client changed the brief three times on set and we still delivered. I have photographed faces, stories, and moments across Lagos and beyond, building a body of work and a reputation through consistency, quality, and the refusal to deliver anything less than my best.

But here is what I kept noticing: the same mistakes, over and over. Young photographers buying expensive cameras but shooting on auto. Talented creatives undercharging because nobody taught them how to price their work. Skilled shooters with no contracts, no financial plan, no idea what their work actually costs them.

So I wrote the book I wish I had when I started. A complete guide from zero to professional, grounded entirely in Nigerian reality. Not translated from some American course. Written here, for here. With examples from Balogun Market and Oshodi, pricing in Naira, equipment guides for Computer Village, and business strategies tested on Lagos roads.

Then I turned that book into this masterclass.

I priced it at ₦15,000 deliberately. Not ₦150,000. Not ₦75,000. Fifteen thousand Naira. Because this is not about extracting maximum profit. This is my way of pouring back into the community that shaped me. Every serious Nigerian creative should be able to afford access to this knowledge. The same knowledge that took me years and hundreds of thousands of Naira in mistakes to learn.

16 chapters. 4 structured parts. 60+ real-world examples pulled from actual Nigerian shoots. 45+ practice exercises you can do with whatever camera you currently own. Pricing tables in Naira. Contract templates. A complete 90-day business launch plan. It is all here.
